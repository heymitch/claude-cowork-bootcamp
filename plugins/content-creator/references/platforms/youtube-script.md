# YouTube Script Platform Guide

## Format Overview

- **Target length:** 8-15 minutes for most content (roughly 1,200-2,200 words at conversational pace)
- **Short-form (Shorts/Reels):** Under 60 seconds (100-150 words)
- **Language:** Spoken, not written. Short sentences. Fragments are fine. Contractions always.
- **Hook:** First 30 seconds is everything. If you lose them there, they're gone.
- **Structure:** Hook → Problem → Solution → Proof → CTA
- **Sections:** Timestamp-friendly. Each section should have a clear start so editors can add chapters.

---

## The Hook (First 30 Seconds)

The hook is the most important part of any YouTube video. It has one job: make the viewer decide they're watching this video right now instead of something else.

### Hook Structure

```
[Pattern interrupt — start mid-thought, mid-action, or with a bold claim. Never "Hey guys, welcome back."]

[What's in it for them — tell them the one thing they'll know or be able to do by the end.]

[Why you (optional) — if relevant, establish why you have credibility on this topic. 1-2 sentences max.]
```

### Hook Patterns That Work

1. **The Result First:** "I made $47,000 from one YouTube video. Here's exactly how I did it."
2. **The Counterintuitive:** "The advice everyone gives about [topic] is wrong. And I can prove it."
3. **The Specific Promise:** "By the end of this video, you'll know exactly how to [specific outcome]."
4. **The Story Drop:** "Two years ago, I got fired. That turned out to be the best thing that ever happened to my business."
5. **The Direct Challenge:** "If you're struggling with [X], you're probably making this one mistake."

**Rule:** Never open with "Hey guys, today we're going to talk about X." Skip the preamble. Start with the thing.

## Full Script Structure

### Tutorial / How-To (8-12 min)

```
[HOOK — 30-60 seconds]
Pattern interrupt → promise → credibility if needed

[THE PROBLEM — 1-2 minutes]
Name the specific struggle. Make the viewer feel seen.
"You've probably experienced [specific frustrating scenario]. Here's why that keeps happening."

[THE SETUP / CONTEXT — 1-2 minutes]
Why does this problem exist? What's the underlying reason?
Keep it brief — get to the solution.

[THE SOLUTION — 4-6 minutes]
Break into 3-5 steps or principles. Each step gets:
- Clear label (Step 1: Do This)
- Brief explanation
- Concrete example or demonstration
- One sentence on why it matters

[PROOF — 1-2 minutes]
Show it works. Case study, before/after, your own results.
"When I applied this, [specific measurable outcome]."

[CTA — 30-45 seconds]
Subscribe prompt + related video or playlist + any offer or lead magnet
Don't do all three — pick the most important one and go deep on it.
```

### Story / Opinion (8-15 min)

```
[HOOK — 30-60 seconds]
The most compelling moment in the story or the boldest version of your take.

[SETUP — 1-2 minutes]
Who were you before this happened? Context without overexplaining.

[CONFLICT / THE THING — 3-4 minutes]
The event, decision, or idea. Be specific. Use real details.

[RESOLUTION — 2-3 minutes]
What happened? What changed? What did you do?

[THE LESSON — 2-3 minutes]
Connect the story to the viewer's life. "Here's what this means for you."

[CTA — 30-45 seconds]
Same rules as above — one clear ask.
```

## Script Formatting Rules

### Language

- Write how you talk. Read every line out loud before you commit to it.
- Contractions always: "you're" not "you are," "I've" not "I have."
- Short sentences. A sentence over 20 words should probably be two sentences.
- Fragments are fine: "Not complicated. Just consistent."
- Rhetorical questions work: "So why does everyone keep doing it wrong?" (Pause. Answer it.)

### Pacing Markers

Use these in the script to help with delivery:

- `[PAUSE]` — for emphasis after a key point
- `[CUT TO B-ROLL: X]` — notes for editing
- `[SHOW SCREEN: X]` — for tutorials requiring screen share
- `[BEAT]` — short pause, less dramatic than PAUSE

### Section Headers

Use clear headers in the script so editors can build chapters:

```
## SECTION: [Section Name] (~[X] minutes)
```

These become timestamps in the video description.

## Length Guide by Content Type

| Content Type | Length | Word Count |
|-------------|--------|-----------|
| YouTube Short | Under 60 sec | 100-150 words |
| Quick take / Opinion | 3-5 min | 450-750 words |
| Standard tutorial | 8-12 min | 1,200-1,800 words |
| Deep dive | 12-20 min | 1,800-3,000 words |
| Long-form essay / documentary | 20+ min | 3,000+ words |

## CTA Patterns That Work

- "If you want to go deeper on this, I made a video on [related topic] — it's linked right here."
- "Subscribe if you want more videos like this — I post every [day] on [topic]."
- "I wrote about this in detail in my newsletter — link is in the description."
- "[Lead magnet offer] — I put together a free [resource] that covers [benefit]. Link's below."

**Rule:** Pick ONE call to action per video. More than one and the viewer does none of them.

---

## What NOT to Do

- Don't start with "Hey guys, welcome back to my channel."
- Don't spend 2 minutes explaining what the video will cover. Just cover it.
- Don't pad with filler to hit a length target. 8 tight minutes beats 15 padded ones.
- Don't write for reading. If it sounds like an essay, rewrite it so it sounds like talking.
- Don't forget to script the CTA. Winging it leads to awkward endings.
- Don't bury the best content at the end. If you have a great insight, put it in the first 3 minutes AND return to it at the end.

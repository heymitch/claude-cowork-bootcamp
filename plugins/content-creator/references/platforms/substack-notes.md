# Substack Notes Platform Guide

## Format Overview

- **Length:** 1-4 sentences. Occasionally up to a short paragraph (5-6 sentences max).
- **Tone:** Conversational. Feels like a thought you had, not a post you wrote.
- **Purpose:** Drive traffic to your newsletter. Create ambient presence with your audience.
- **Images:** Optional but effective. A single image can anchor the note.
- **Links:** Can link to your newsletter, a relevant article, or leave it linkless.

---

## What a Substack Note Is

Think of it as a text message to your audience. Not a mini-essay. Not a LinkedIn post. A thought you'd share with a friend over coffee.

Good notes feel like:
- "I've been thinking about X and here's the one thing that clicked."
- "Quick observation from this week."
- "I wrote about this in today's newsletter — [one sentence summary]."
- "Something I keep seeing that nobody's talking about."

Bad notes feel like:
- "Excited to announce that my newsletter just dropped! Check it out!"
- A LinkedIn post pasted directly into Notes.
- An ad for your newsletter.

---

## Structure Patterns

### The Observation

```
[1-2 sentences stating a specific observation or pattern you've noticed.]

[Optional: 1-2 sentences adding context or your take.]
```

Example:
> The writers getting the most newsletter growth right now aren't the ones with the best content. They're the ones with the clearest one-sentence pitch for who their newsletter is for.

### The Tease

```
[1-2 sentences teasing what's in your latest newsletter.]

[Optional: Direct link to the issue.]
```

Example:
> This week's newsletter is about the mistake most people make when they hit writer's block. It's not what you think it is. [LINK]

### The Micro-Take

```
[One bold or contrarian statement.]
```

Example:
> Consistency is overrated. Showing up with something worth saying matters more than showing up every Tuesday at 9am.

### The Question

```
[1-2 sentences posing a genuine question to your audience.]
```

Example:
> What's the last newsletter you actually looked forward to receiving? I'm curious what it was and why.

---

## Rules

- **Short.** If it's longer than 5 sentences, it's a newsletter issue, not a note.
- **Feels typed, not crafted.** Notes that feel polished lose the intimacy that makes them work.
- **One idea.** Don't try to make two points. One thought, expressed clearly, and out.
- **No clichés.** "Grateful for this journey" belongs nowhere near Substack Notes.
- **Drive to the newsletter naturally.** The note should make someone want to subscribe, but don't pitch directly. Let the quality of the thought do the work.
- **Post frequency:** 3-5 per week is a sweet spot for building presence without fatigue. Daily is possible if you have enough genuine thoughts — don't manufacture them.

---

## What NOT to Do

- Don't repurpose a full LinkedIn post into Notes. The formats are different.
- Don't start with "In today's newsletter..." unless you're linking directly.
- Don't add 10 hashtags. Notes doesn't use hashtags that way.
- Don't turn every note into a newsletter ad. Mix in pure observations.
- Don't write in a "broadcast" voice. It should sound like you, not a brand.

# Newsletter Platform Guide

## Format Overview

- **Typical length:** 500-1,500 words
- **Structure:** Hook → Story/Setup → Insight → Evidence/Depth → Takeaway → CTA
- **Subject line:** Under 60 characters, front-load the most important word
- **Preview text:** Under 90 characters — it's a second subject line, complement don't repeat

---

## The Newsletter Fork: What Kind Are You Building?

### Curation Newsletters

**Core benefit:** Save the reader time. Compress a lot of information into a quick read.

Best for: Fast-moving categories (tech, markets, pop culture). Writers curating from other sources.

Examples: Morning Brew, The Hustle, Robinhood Snacks — they all promise a time commitment.

**Monetization:** Mostly ads. Volume plays.

### Original Thinking Newsletters

**Core benefit:** Accelerate the reader's expertise. Go deep on one topic.

Best for: Slower-moving categories (writing, strategy, psychology). Writers with domain expertise whose opinions are genuinely valuable.

Examples: Category Pirates, Stratechery, The Generalist — you sit down with coffee and actually read them.

**Monetization:** Can go paid. Lower subscriber counts, exponentially lower churn.

**Rule:** Know which one is your PRIMARY value. Mixing is fine — but 99% of the time readers subscribe for one or the other.

## The Pinpoint Writing Framework

Before picking a format, answer these 5 questions:

1. What problem am I solving?
2. Whose problem am I solving?
3. What benefit am I unlocking?
4. What emotion am I creating?
5. What action am I encouraging?

Write to 1 specific person to solve 1 specific problem. Too specific is almost impossible at internet scale.

---

## Format Decision Tree

Ask one question at a time:

1. **One big idea you can't stop thinking about?** → Essay Style
2. **Found 5+ great links/resources this week?** → Curated Links
3. **One big idea AND great links?** → Hybrid
4. **List of quick insights on one theme?** → Listicle
5. **Readers asking you questions?** → Q&A / Mailbag
6. **Clear step-by-step to teach?** → Original Thinking Template

If none fit → default to Essay.

## Format Skeletons

### Essay Style (800-1,500 words)

```
[Hook — 1-2 sentences. Bold claim, surprising stat, or "I was wrong about..." opener.]

[Story/Setup — 2-3 paragraphs. A specific moment, not abstract setup.]

[The Insight — The point. Say it directly.]

[Evidence/Depth — 2-3 paragraphs. Examples, data, comparisons.]

[Takeaway — 1 paragraph. One concrete action.]

[CTA — 1-2 sentences. Reply, share, or see you next week.]
```

### Curated Links (400-800 words)

```
[Intro — 2-3 sentences. What's the theme? Why these links?]

**1. [Link Title](URL)**
[Your take — 2-3 sentences. Your OPINION on why this matters, not a summary.]

[Repeat for 3-5 links]

[Closing thought — 1 paragraph. The thread connecting these. CTA.]
```

### Hybrid (600-1,200 words)

```
[Hook + Main Essay — 3-5 paragraphs. Your original piece, shorter than full essay.]

[Takeaway — 1 paragraph with a concrete action.]

---

**Also Worth Reading**

**1. [Link Title](URL)** — [1-2 sentence take.]
[Repeat for 2-3 links]

[CTA]
```

### Listicle (600-1,000 words)

```
[Hook — 1-2 sentences. Name the theme. Why now?]

**1. [Insight title]**
[2-3 sentences. Example or reason.]

[Repeat for 5-10 items. Vary item length — some 1-liners, some 3 sentences.]

[Wrap-up — Meta-insight: what do all items have in common? CTA.]
```

### Q&A / Mailbag (600-1,000 words)

```
[Intro — 2-3 sentences. "You asked, I'm answering."]

---

**Q: "[Reader's question]"**

[Your answer — 2-4 paragraphs. Specific, real opinion.]

[Repeat for 3-5 questions]

[Closing — Invite more questions. Tell them how to submit. CTA.]
```

---

## General Rules

- **First sentence matters most.** Boring opener = nothing else matters.
- **One idea per paragraph.** Two things in one paragraph → split it.
- **End with energy.** Last line should make them want to come back.
- **White space is your friend.** Short paragraphs. Break up walls of text.
- **No filler intros.** Never start with "In this week's newsletter, I'll be covering..."

---

## Viral Loop: Growing Your List

- Write a social post (Twitter, LinkedIn) that delivers standalone value
- Link to the newsletter for the expanded version with more depth, stories, action steps
- Repeat

Your newsletter doesn't need to be 100% net-new content. A tweet about morning routines becomes "7 Things You Need In Your Morning Routine" with frameworks and examples.

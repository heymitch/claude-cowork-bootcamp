# LinkedIn Platform Guide

## Format Overview

- **Max length:** 3,000 characters (~500 words)
- **Sweet spot:** 800-1,500 characters for most posts
- **Hook:** The first line is everything — it shows before "see more." Make it stop the scroll.
- **Paragraphs:** 1-2 sentences each, separated by blank lines. No walls of text.
- **Hashtags:** 3 max. More than 3 looks like spam.
- **Tone:** Professional but personal. You're an expert who also has a life.
- **CTA:** Always end with a question or a clear next step.

---

## Structure Patterns

### Story Arc (personal lesson)

```
[Hook — Bold claim, surprising result, or "I used to think X. I was wrong."]

[Setup — 2-3 short paragraphs. What happened? Set the scene with a specific moment.]

[Turning point — What changed? The insight you had.]

[Lesson — The takeaway for the reader. Be direct. Say the thing.]

[CTA — Question to drive comments or soft pitch for next step]
```

### Point-of-View Post (opinion or take)

```
[Hook — Your contrarian or bold opinion, stated plainly.]

[Evidence — 3-5 short paragraphs. Why do you believe this? Examples, data, experience.]

[Implication — What does this mean for the reader? What should they do differently?]

[CTA — "Agree? Disagree? Let me know in the comments." or similar]
```

### List Post (tactical advice)

```
[Hook — "Here are [N] things I wish I'd known about X."]

[Item 1] (one short paragraph)

[Item 2]

[Item 3]

[Item 4]

[Item 5]

[Closing — The meta-point. What do all items have in common? CTA.]
```

---

## Hook Patterns for LinkedIn

The first line must work before "see more." These patterns perform:

- **Contrarian claim:** "Most [common thing] is a waste of time."
- **Personal story opener:** "I lost my biggest client. Best thing that happened to me."
- **Data point:** "I've reviewed 50+ LinkedIn profiles this year. Here's the #1 mistake."
- **Bold declaration:** "Cold outreach doesn't work. Warm outreach does. Here's the difference."
- **Question:** "Why do 90% of LinkedIn posts get under 1,000 impressions?"

**Rule:** Never start with "I am excited to share..." or "Great news!" Those are the two most-scrolled-past openers on the platform.

---

## Formatting Rules

- One blank line between every paragraph. Always.
- Short sentences. If a sentence runs past 20 words, split it.
- No bullet points inside the main body unless it's a list post. Bullets kill momentum on narrative posts.
- Bold sparingly — only for the most important phrase per post, not every other line.
- Emojis: only if the user's voice profile shows they use them. Max 1 per paragraph if so.
- No hashtags in the middle of text. Put them at the very end.

---

## Length Guidance by Post Type

| Post Type | Characters |
|-----------|-----------|
| Quick thought / Substack note style | 300-600 |
| Story or lesson | 800-1,500 |
| Deep-dive or opinion piece | 1,500-2,500 |
| Long-form article (rare) | 2,500-3,000 |

**Most performing posts are 800-1,500 characters.** Longer doesn't mean more engagement — it means the hook needs to be stronger to earn the read.

---

## CTA Patterns That Work

- "What's your take? Drop it in the comments."
- "Agree or disagree — would love to hear from you."
- "If this resonates, share it with someone who needs to hear it."
- "I wrote about this in depth in my newsletter — link in comments."
- Soft pitch: "I help [audience] do [outcome]. DM me if you want to talk."

---

## What NOT to Do

- Don't end every post with 30 hashtags.
- Don't post engagement bait: "Like this if you agree!"
- Don't start with "In this post, I will be sharing..."
- Don't use corporate speak: "synergize," "leverage," "paradigm shift."
- Don't make every post a humble brag disguised as advice.
- Don't repost the same article headline and nothing else.

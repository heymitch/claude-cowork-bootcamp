# Launch Checklist

Everything you need to do before, during, and after launching your digital product.

## Pre-Launch (1-2 Weeks Before)

### Product Ready
- [ ] Product is complete and proofread (read it out loud — you will catch errors)
- [ ] Cover image or design is done
- [ ] Product file is exported in final format (PDF, ZIP, etc.)

### Sales Page Ready
- [ ] Product page is live on your platform (Gumroad, Stripe, Lemon Squeezy)
- [ ] Description is written with: teaser, "what you'll learn" bullets, pricing
- [ ] Preview pages or sample content is uploaded
- [ ] Pricing is set (see pricing-guide.md for help)
- [ ] Cover image is uploaded to product page

### Content Ready
- [ ] 3 test posts are written and scheduled (demand validation — see topic-validator)
- [ ] Launch day social posts are written (at least 3 platform versions)
- [ ] Email draft to your list is written (if you have a list)

### Distribution Ready
- [ ] Friends and colleagues are notified — they are your first buyers and amplifiers
- [ ] Checkout flow tested end-to-end (buy your own product with a test card or 100% discount code)
- [ ] Download link works after purchase
- [ ] Receipt email looks right

## Launch Day

### Morning (8-10am your audience's timezone)
- [ ] Post launch announcement on your primary platform
- [ ] Pin the post to your profile (if the platform allows it)
- [ ] Send email to your list (if applicable)

### Midday (12-2pm)
- [ ] Share on secondary platforms (cross-post with platform-native formatting)
- [ ] Share in any relevant communities, Slack groups, Discord servers (where allowed)
- [ ] DM 5-10 people who you know would genuinely benefit (not spammy — targeted)

### Afternoon (3-5pm)
- [ ] Engage with every comment and share on your launch post
- [ ] Reply to every DM about the product
- [ ] Reshare or repost with a different angle ("5 people grabbed this in the first 2 hours" or highlight a specific section)

### Evening (7-9pm)
- [ ] Share first results or social proof (even "3 people bought in the first hour" works)
- [ ] Thank anyone who shared or promoted your product publicly
- [ ] Post a "behind the scenes" or "why I made this" story

## Post-Launch (Weeks 1-4)

### Week 1
- [ ] Create 2-3 evergreen CTA posts (not "buy my thing" — share insights from the product, link in bio or comments)
- [ ] DM people who engaged with launch posts to thank them
- [ ] Ask your first 5 buyers for a one-line testimonial
- [ ] Fix anything broken (typos, links, formatting issues buyers report)

### Week 2
- [ ] Write a "what I learned launching this" post (meta-content about the launch performs well)
- [ ] Add testimonials to your product page
- [ ] Create 2 more content pieces that naturally reference the product

### Weeks 3-4
- [ ] Set up recurring promotion: mention the product naturally in related content
- [ ] Create a "resources" or "start here" section on your profile that includes the product
- [ ] Plan your next product based on what buyers ask for
- [ ] Review analytics: where did buyers come from? Double down on that channel.

## Timing Tips

### Best Days to Launch
- **Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday** — highest engagement on most platforms
- **Avoid Monday** — people are catching up from the weekend
- **Avoid Friday** — people are checking out for the weekend

### Best Times to Post
- **LinkedIn:** 7-9am or 12-1pm (business hours, local time)
- **Twitter/X:** 8-10am or 5-7pm (commute times)
- **Email:** Tuesday or Thursday, 7-9am

### Launch Week Cadence
- Day 1 (Tuesday): Launch announcement
- Day 2 (Wednesday): Behind-the-scenes or "why I built this"
- Day 3 (Thursday): Share a key insight from the product
- Day 5 (Saturday): "X people grabbed this — here's what they're saying"
- Day 7 (Monday): Evergreen CTA with a different angle

## Common Launch Mistakes

1. **Launching on Friday afternoon.** Nobody is buying digital products at 4pm on Friday.
2. **One post and done.** You need 5-10 posts in the first week, not one.
3. **Not testing checkout.** If the buy button is broken, you will not know until someone tells you. Test it yourself.
4. **Waiting until it is perfect.** Ship it at 80%. Fix the last 20% after launch based on real feedback.
5. **Not asking for testimonials.** Your first 5 buyers are your marketing team. Ask them.

# LTL Frameworks Reference

Detailed templates and frameworks from the 0 to $1 digital product curriculum. Referenced by the digital-product skill.

## The 8-Question Outline Framework

Answer these to have 80% of the work done:

1. **Who is this for, specifically?**
2. **What problem of theirs are you solving?**
3. **What benefit gets unlocked when this problem gets solved?**
4. **How "expensive" is this problem?** How are you providing the solution for a fraction of the normal cost?
5. **What myths keep people from solving this problem?**
6. **What was the moment in your life when you first experienced this problem?** How did you overcome it?
7. **What 3rd-party examples, case studies, or stories prove it's possible?** What amazing things happen when people overcome this?
8. **What 3-7 action steps/framework will you walk the reader through?**

## The 1+1+1 Product Naming Framework

- **1 Product** — what's the topic?
- **For 1 Audience** — who specifically?
- **Solving 1 Problem** — what transformation?

Main Title = Product + Audience
Subtitle = Problem/Transformation

## 10 Proven Sections of a Digital Product

### Section 1: Dear WHO
- **You, You, You**: List of 5+ "who you might be" profiles
- **Stuck In Struggle City**: List of 5+ biggest problems your target audience faces
- **Blame Game**: Give the audience something else to blame (other than themselves)
- **3 Big Myths**: The advice most people give to solve this problem
- **The Unlikely Answer**: Your counterintuitive but proven answer
- **Benefits Of The Road Less Traveled**: List 5+ rewards they unlock
- **Future, Reimagined**: What their life looks like "on the other side"
- **Call To Adventure**: "Are you ready to transform?"

### Section 2: I Was Once Where You Are
- **Origin Story**: When were you stuck in Struggle City?
- **Bad Advice**: What did other "experts" tell you to do?
- **Quicksand**: 5+ bad outcomes the bad advice led to
- **Dark Night Of The Soul**: The low point — when you thought of giving up
- **The Moment Of Change**: When you turned the corner, what happened
- **Success Story**: What you did and achieved — your credibility
- **I Did, You Can Too**: Reverse engineer your journey into steps/frameworks for the reader

### Sections 3-7: Steps 1-5
Each step follows this structure:
- **No Shortcuts**: Why they can't skip this and shouldn't want to
- **Step**: The specific action in detail
- **Motivational Reminder**: Why this matters and what it leads to
- **Caution Signs**: 3-5 common mistakes to avoid
- **Examples**: 3+ real examples of execution
- **Template**: An easy-to-use outline or template to get started

### Section 8: Before/After
- **Transformation Celebration**: Recap the journey, show how far they've come
- **Case Studies**: 3+ quick stories about others who did the same
- **Continuous Improvement**: 5+ action steps to keep improving

### Section 9: Frequently Asked Questions
- 10+ FAQs with 1-3 paragraph answers each
- Cover common confusions, edge cases, and "what ifs"

### Section 10: CTAs
- **Thank You**: Thank the reader for investing their time
- **Resources**: Link follow-up resources, free guides, newsletters
- **Questions**: Give contact info for follow-ups
- **1:1 Pitch**: Upsell to working together directly

## Lean Writing Validation Process

Before building the full product, test with "Lean Writing":

1. Take your 3 candidate topics
2. Write about them MORE on social
3. Pay attention to:
   - Questions people ask (strongest signal)
   - Response quality ("I would love this!" vs silence)
   - Engagement relative to your audience
   - How excited YOU get about sharing this idea
4. After testing, 1 topic will emerge as the winner
5. That's the one to build into a digital product

**Key insight**: Comments > Likes > Views for product validation. Questions from readers = monetizable niche.

## Product Description Template (for Gumroad)

### Part 1: Teaser
- What's the industry and why does it matter (include stats if available)
- What problem are you solving and why is it urgent
- Your 30-second bio — why you're credible on this subject

### Part 2: "In This eBook, You Will Learn"
- What walkthrough guide(s) are you giving them?
- What templates can they immediately use?
- What mental shifts will happen — and what outcomes do those lead to?

### Pricing Guidelines
- Short eBooks / beginner guides: $8-15
- Comprehensive guides with templates: $20-50
- Premium with frameworks + templates + case studies: $50-100
- Rule: reader needs to see 10x the value of the price

## 4 Product Design Styles

1. **Minimalist** — Black/white, 1-2 colors, bold font. (Jack Butcher style.) Best for: budget-conscious, "less is more" positioning.
2. **Operating System** — Product looks like software bundle. (Justin Welsh style.) Best for: course-like or system-like products.
3. **Stock Photo Professional** — Clean stock imagery + professional layout. Best for: quick, polished, cost-effective.
4. **Original Cover** — Custom design by professional. $500-5000. Best for: flagship products, differentiation.

# Write the Course — Strategy Frameworks

Condensed from Ship 30 for 30's "Write the Course: Create, Launch, and Monetize Your First Online Course in 30 Days." These are the strategy decisions that must happen BEFORE you build.

## The Philosophy

You are NOT creating an educational product. Nobody wants to learn. Learning is boring, takes a long time, and is delayed gratification.

Instead, you are selling people 3 things:

1. **Action Steps** — Things to DO, not things to learn. The more you teach, the more eyes glaze over. The more you force them to DO, the more engaged they are.
2. **Templates** — Make the DOING as easy as possible. Templates reduce fear and friction of getting started and remaining consistent.
3. **Accountability & Support** — Community, daily/weekly recaps, active participation. People need to know you're there.

**Why this matters:** Online courses have 5-10% completion rates. Your product's success = completion rate = word-of-mouth = revenue. 100x better for 10 people to buy and 9 finish than 1,000 to buy and 9 finish.

**The test:** Whenever you find yourself "teaching," challenge yourself: what's the ACTION? What can they DO right now?

## Framework 1: Superniche Positioning

Don't build a product. Dominate a niche.

**The difference is Grand Canyon-sized:**
- Bad: "How to be more productive"
- Good: "How to manage your life and business inside Notion" (Marie Poulin — 950 students, $500K revenue in 2021)

Your product name IS your niche name. It should make immediately clear:
- **WHO** this is for
- **WHAT** it's about
- **WHY** it matters

**How to find your Superniche:**
1. Mine your analytics — sort by Comments (not Views). Comments = "I'm interested enough to engage" = most likely to buy.
2. Ask your audience directly — "If I built this, would you find it valuable?"
3. Research the market — search "[Topic] Course", look at "People Also Ask." Find what's NOT being done.
4. Confirm you're the right person — "I did it, and you can too" is enough credibility.
5. Confirm it's a DOING product, not a THINKING product.

**"Too niche" = neon sign screaming GIANT OPPORTUNITY.** Every time someone says that, they're revealing a gap.

## Framework 2: The Main Offer

The main offer is 90% of the work. Three questions:
1. What's the big promise?
2. What's the primary pain point you solve?
3. What outcome do you generate?

**How to find your Main Offer:**

**Step 1:** List 7+ pain points your product solves. Be hyper-specific. Not "people want to succeed" — that's vague. "Struggle to understand how to file taxes 1-2 years late" — that's specific enough to sell itself.

**Step 2:** Choose the pain point with the most urgency. Signals:
- People complain about this everywhere on social media
- You get asked the same question over and over
- You've experienced it yourself and found a faster way through

**Step 3:** Stress-test it. Write about it online. Talk to potential customers. If the response is lukewarm, better to know now.

**Pro Tip:** Pick the pain point tied to a financial outcome. Courses that force DOING and unlock financial results are much easier to sell than courses that "teach."

## Framework 3: The 7 Problems

Your product solves your Main Offer (#1 pain point) PLUS 6+ supporting problems. The best products solve all three types:

**"Understanding" Problems** — Changing how they think:
- Make a choice (stuck between A or B)
- Keep going or stop (patience vs. pivot)
- Abandon a myth (faulty belief they can't let go of)
- Feel confident in their decision (need to hear it from you)

**"Actionable" Problems** — The DOING (core of your product):
- Put theory into practice (know the answer, can't execute)
- Fix mistakes (same pitfalls over and over)
- Increase efficiencies (know WHAT but not HOW to do it fast)
- The right way, the first time (don't want to waste effort)

**"Emotional" Problems** — What's holding them back beneath the surface:
- Overcome a fear (know what to do, need someone to push them)
- Believe in themselves (have expertise, lack self-confidence)
- Trust the process (impatient, need accountability)

**The best products combine all 3.** Most creators only solve one type and wonder why it doesn't land.

## Framework 4: The 7 Outcomes

Mirror your 7 Problems. For each problem solved, name the outcome unlocked.

Format: "[Verb] + [specific result]"
- "Stop overthinking and start writing"
- "Generate hundreds of content ideas"
- "Publish 30 pieces in 30 days"
- "Master the fundamentals of digital writing"
- "Join a community of like-minded thinkers"

These become your bullet points on the sales page and your product's table of contents.

## Framework 5: Pricing (Set Price First)

**Don't build then price. Price then build to that price.**

Ask: "How much money am I trying to make? What price point × how many buyers = that number?"

- $10/month goal: 100 buyers at $10, or 10 at $100, or 1 at $1,000
- All three require the same level of effort. Cheap ≠ more buyers.

**Three pricing tiers:**
- **Beginner ($50-$1,000):** Low-pressure entry. Beginners dipping toes. Action steps + templates.
- **Intermediate ($1,000-$5,000):** Proven transformation. Community + accountability + live elements.
- **Advanced ($5,000+):** Business-level outcomes. Done-with-you. Revenue unlocks.

**The rule:** Price should feel cheap compared to the outcome. "This is insanely cheap for the value" = signal to raise price.

**Start low, prove demand, raise later.** Ship 30 started at $99. Raised to $149, $199, $249, $299+ as value increased and customers confirmed it was "too cheap."

## Framework 6: Origin Story

Every product needs a "why you" story. Structure:

1. **When you were stuck** — the same problem your audience has now
2. **The bad advice** — what everyone told you to do (and why it didn't work)
3. **The low point** — the moment it got worst
4. **The turning point** — what changed, what you discovered
5. **Your success** — what happened when you applied the new approach
6. **"I did it, you can too"** — the bridge from your story to their future

This becomes Section 2 of your product AND the emotional core of your marketing.

## The Strategy Brief (Output Format)

After working through these frameworks, you should have:

```
Superniche: [specific audience] + [specific tool/method/angle]
Main Offer: [1 big promise solving 1 urgent pain point]
7 Problems: [list — mix of Understanding, Actionable, Emotional]
7 Outcomes: [mirror of problems — what unlocks when each is solved]
Price tier: [Beginner/Intermediate/Advanced] — $[amount]
Origin hook: [1 sentence — when you were stuck + what changed]
Method name: [your unique name for your approach, if applicable]
```

This brief feeds directly into outline-builder, title-generator, and content-writer.

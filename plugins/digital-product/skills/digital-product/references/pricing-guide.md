# Pricing Guide

How to price your digital product. Practical tiers, psychology, and platform fees.

## Pricing Tiers

### Mini Guide ($5-15)
The impulse buy. Quick-reference checklists, templates, one-pagers. Under 20 pages.

**What to include:**
- One clear framework or checklist
- 1-3 templates they can use immediately
- Short intro (no origin story needed)
- Clean formatting with headers and bullet points

**What justifies the price:**
- Saves them 2+ hours of research
- Actionable in under 30 minutes
- Solves one specific problem

**Examples:** "The 5-Step Cold Email Template," "Weekly Content Calendar Template," "Freelancer Rate Calculator"

**Production time:** 2-5 hours

---

### Standard Guide ($15-50)
The no-brainer. Full how-to guides, playbooks, frameworks. 30-80 pages.

**What to include:**
- Complete walkthrough of a process (start to finish)
- 3-5 templates or worksheets
- Real examples and case studies
- Origin story and credibility section
- FAQ section

**What justifies the price:**
- Replaces a $200+ course or $500+ consultant
- Step-by-step — they can follow it without guessing
- Includes templates they would otherwise build themselves

**Examples:** "The LinkedIn Growth Playbook," "How to Land Your First Freelance Client," "The Creator's Guide to Digital Products"

**Production time:** 10-30 hours

---

### Premium Product ($50-150)
The serious investment. Comprehensive courses, bundled templates + video, multi-part systems.

**What to include:**
- Everything in Standard, plus:
- Video walkthroughs or screen recordings
- Swipe files and done-for-you templates (10+)
- Community access or bonus Q&A
- Updates for 6-12 months

**What justifies the price:**
- Saves them weeks of trial and error
- Multiple formats (reading + watching + doing)
- Ongoing value through updates

**Examples:** "The Complete Content System," "SaaS Launch Toolkit," "Freelancer Business-in-a-Box"

**Production time:** 40-80 hours

---

### High-Ticket ($150-500+)
The transformation. Done-with-you programs, cohorts, extensive templates + community access.

**What to include:**
- Everything in Premium, plus:
- Live calls or coaching component
- Personalized feedback on their work
- Private community with ongoing support
- Accountability structure

**What justifies the price:**
- They get access to YOU, not just your content
- Personalized guidance beats generic advice
- Community creates network effects

**Examples:** "12-Week Cohort Program," "Done-With-You Launch Sprint," "Mastermind + Toolkit Bundle"

**Production time:** 80-200 hours (including delivery)

## Pricing Psychology

### Price Anchoring
Show the premium option first. When someone sees the $150 product, the $29 version looks like a steal. On your sales page, list packages top-down: Premium > Standard > Mini.

### Odd Pricing ($9 vs $10)
- Under $20: odd pricing works ($9, $19, $27). It feels cheaper.
- Over $50: round numbers work better ($50, $99, $149). They signal quality.
- Exception: $0 to $1 — your first product should be priced to sell, not to maximize revenue. Pick a price that makes you uncomfortable because it's too low. That's the right price for product #1.

### The Free vs Paid Threshold
Free content builds audience. Paid content builds business. The moment you charge $1, you become a seller. That's the hardest dollar. Price your first product low enough that the barrier is psychological, not financial. $9 is perfect for a first product.

### Volume vs Price
- At $9, you need 111 sales for $1,000
- At $29, you need 35 sales for $1,000
- At $49, you need 21 sales for $1,000
- At $99, you need 11 sales for $1,000

Small audience (under 1,000 followers)? Price higher. Large audience (10,000+)? You can price lower and make it up in volume.

## Platform Fees

| Platform | Fee | On a $29 product |
|----------|-----|-------------------|
| Gumroad | 10% flat | $2.90 — you keep $26.10 |
| Stripe | 2.9% + $0.30 | $1.14 — you keep $27.86 |
| Lemon Squeezy | 5% + $0.50 | $1.95 — you keep $27.05 |

Factor fees into your pricing. If you want to net $25, price at $28 on Gumroad or $26 on Stripe.

## Decision Tree: How to Price Your First Product

Answer 3 questions:

**1. How long is it?**
- Under 20 pages / 1 template → $5-15
- 30-80 pages / multiple templates → $15-50
- Course-level with video/community → $50-150

**2. How big is your audience?**
- Under 500 followers → price low ($9-19), focus on getting buyers
- 500-5,000 followers → mid-range ($19-49), you have enough reach
- 5,000+ followers → price with confidence ($29-99+)

**3. Is this your first product?**
- Yes → go lower. Your goal is your first sale, not maximum revenue. $9-19.
- No → price based on value. You have proof people will buy from you.

**The formula:** Start low, prove demand, raise price later. Nobody ever regretted launching at $9 and raising to $29 after 50 sales.

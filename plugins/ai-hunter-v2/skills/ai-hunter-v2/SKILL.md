# AI Hunter 2.0

Deterministic AI pattern detection. Two independent scanners, combined score. Built from 200+ experiments.

## How to Use

Paste your content and say "ai hunter" or "scan this for ai patterns."

## What It Catches

### Critical (15 pts each)
- Negative parallelism (defining through what something ISN'T)
- Throat-clearing ("Here's what/why/how", "The truth is")
- Cringe questions ("Sound familiar?", "And the wildest part?", performed empathy)

### High (10-12 pts each)
- Rule of three staccato (three short parallel fragments)
- Dashes (em, en, spaced — zero tolerance)
- Formulaic hooks ("Most people", "Nobody talks about")

### Medium (8 pts each)
- Corporate jargon (leverage, framework, optimize, landscape)
- AI transitions (furthermore, moreover, in conclusion)
- Even paragraph lengths

### Low (5-6 pts each)
- Flat rhythm (three consecutive similar-length sentences)
- Monotone sentence length
- Exclamation overuse

## Scoring

- A (90-100): Publishable
- B (80-89): 1-2 sentences need attention
- C (70-79): 3-5 fixes needed
- D (60-69): Major revision
- F (< 60): Start over

## Key Research Findings

1. "Be creative" beats rule stacks for generation
2. GPTZero is the wrong primary metric. Human-detectable patterns matter.
3. Negative parallelism is the deepest AI tell
4. Performed empathy ("That's real. Take a second.") is slop
5. Rule of three is the #1 remaining pattern after dashes and contrast are fixed

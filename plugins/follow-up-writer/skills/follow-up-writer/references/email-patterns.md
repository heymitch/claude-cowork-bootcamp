# Follow-Up Email Patterns

Four go-to patterns. Match to the context—don't default to the same one every time.

---

## 1. Action-Item Follow-Up (most common)

Use when: the meeting produced commitments, tasks, or deliverables.

**Structure:**
- One-line opener referencing the meeting
- Bullet list: who owes what, by when
- One clear next milestone ("next check-in is X" or "ball is in your court on Y")
- Brief close

**Tone:** Direct. No fluff. The action items are the point.

**Example opener:**
> "Great talking through the project today. Here's what we agreed to so nothing falls through the cracks."

---

## 2. Decision Confirmation

Use when: a key decision was made and both parties need it in writing.

**Structure:**
- State the decision clearly in the first line
- One sentence of context (why this decision was made)
- Any conditions or caveats
- Who needs to act on it and when

**Tone:** Precise and brief. This is the record.

**Example opener:**
> "Wanted to confirm the decision we landed on today so we're aligned going forward."

---

## 3. Next Steps

Use when: the meeting was more exploratory—no hard commitments yet, but clear forward motion.

**Structure:**
- Brief summary of what was covered (2–3 sentences max)
- Numbered next steps with owners
- Timeline or proposed next meeting

**Tone:** Forward-looking. Optimistic but grounded.

**Example opener:**
> "Following up on our conversation—here's how I see the path forward."

---

## 4. Thank You + Value Add

Use when: first meeting, sales call, or relationship-building context where you want to leave something useful.

**Structure:**
- Genuine one-line thanks (no more)
- One specific thing that stood out from the conversation
- One piece of value: a resource, an idea, a connection, or a relevant observation
- Soft next step (no pressure)

**Tone:** Warm but not gushing. The value add does the work—the tone stays professional.

**Example opener:**
> "Thanks for making the time—I walked away with a clearer picture of where you're headed."

---

## Pattern Selection Guide

| Context | Pattern |
|---------|---------|
| Task-heavy meeting | Action-Item |
| Strategic decision made | Decision Confirmation |
| Discovery or planning call | Next Steps |
| First meeting, sales, intro | Thank You + Value Add |
| Unclear | Ask the user before drafting |

# 🧬 Module 2: Extract Your "Voice DNA"

This lesson will walk you through the steps to isolate your unique voice and style.

The first step is to separate your writing voice from your writing tone. Remember, your voice is consistent across all your writing, while tone can change from piece to piece.

To do this, we'll use a specific prompt with AI.

Here's how it works:

## Step 1: Gather 3-5 samples of your best writing.

These should be pieces where you feel your authentic voice shines through.

If you are just getting started, you can also does this with a writer you admire. This will help you understand what you like and don't like. And it will help you to adopt a similar style into your writing.

## Step 2: Feed each sample into the AI using this prompt.

```javascript
In this session we'll consider the difference between a writing voice and a writing tone.

The writing voice is the voice that stays consistent across all writing. The tone changes from piece to piece.

I need your help in understanding my writing voice so that I can create processes that will utilize my writing voice across content I'll generate with you.

I'll give you some examples of my writing and you'll give me a maximum of 10 bullet points (less if you see everything fits in less) of what my writing voice is.

Understood?
```

After inputting each sample, you'll receive a list of voice attributes.

## Step 3: Merge Voice Responses

Now that you have multiple lists of voice attributes, it's time to consolidate them.

Input all the voice attributes you received from the previous step into this prompt:

```javascript
Here is a list of writing voice attributes.

Please remove duplicates and only output unique the unique voice traits in a simple to read bullet point list:

[INSERT VOICE ATTRIBUTES]
```

This will give you a refined list of your core voice traits.

## Step 4: Isolate Your Writing Style

Next, we'll focus on the technical aspects of your writing. Use this prompt:

```javascript
Analyze the WRITING STYLE (Without the tone, nor the voice) based on these criteria:

1. Word Choice: The specific vocabulary and terminology used.

2. Sentence Structure: How sentences are constructed, including length and complexity.

3. Rhythm and Pacing: The flow and tempo of the writing.

4. Point of View: The perspective from which the story is told.

5. Figurative Language: Use of literary devices like metaphors and similes.

6. Imagery: Descriptive language that creates visual images for the reader.
```

Input your writing samples again, one at a time, using this prompt.

## Step 5: Merge Style Responses

Just like with your voice attributes, you'll now consolidate your style attributes.

Input all the style attributes you received from the previous step into this prompt:

```javascript
Here is a list of writing style attributes.

Please remove duplicates and only output the unique style traits in a simple to read bullet point list:

[INSERT STYLE ATTRIBUTES]
```

## Step 6: Create Your Voice DNA Document

Now you have two refined lists: one for voice attributes and one for style attributes. Combine these into a single document.

This is your Voice DNA.

Your Voice DNA document might look something like this:

Voice Attributes:
- Direct and straightforward
- Conversational and engaging

Style Attributes:
- Favors short to medium-length sentences
- Uses contractions for a casual tone
- Employs metaphors related to everyday experiences
- First-person perspective with frequent use of "you" to address the reader

This document is your key to making AI write like you. Whenever you're using AI to generate content, you can include a condensed version of your Voice DNA in your instructions.

For example:

*"Adapt all your responses to this writing style & tone: Direct, conversational, uses personal anecdotes and rhetorical questions. Short to medium sentences, contractions, everyday metaphors, first-person perspective addressing the reader as 'you'."*

As your writing evolves, you can repeat this process to update your Voice DNA.

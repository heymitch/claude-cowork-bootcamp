# ⚙️ Module 8: Engineer Your Tone With The Tone Grid

The Tone Grid is a simple tool that helps you pinpoint your tone and avoid sounding like everyone else. It comes from Will Hoekenga, the copywriter for Toast and Drift.

<video src="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kQuAYe0gmGk"></video>

The Tone Grid has two axes:
- X-axis: Loose vs. Tight copy
- Y-axis: Irreverent vs. Professional

Most writers sit in the middle - the "Neutral Zone." Their writing is structured and friendly. It's nice. But, it's forgettable.

But step into the "Distinct or Extreme Zone," and you'll stand out. Your writing will feel different. And readers will notice.

Think Apple. Tight, polished copy that sells without trying.
*"Unbelievably thin. Incredibly powerful."*

Or Basecamp. Loose, conversational. Like a smart friend giving advice.
*"It's not everything under the sun, just the stuff to get things done."*

Let's look at two different writers:

## Example: Malcolm Gladwell

He lives in the tight, polished corner.
His sentences are crisp and punchy.
*"The tipping point is that magic moment when an idea, trend, or social behavior crosses a threshold, tips, and spreads like wildfire."*

It's clear. It sticks with you.

## Example: David Sedaris

He's in the loose, irreverent area.
*"If you're looking for sympathy you'll find it between shit and syphilis in the dictionary."*

He's casual, unexpected, and memorable.

It's clear where both these writers sit on the Tone Grid.

With this simple tool you can:
- Stand out from other writers
- Stick in readers' minds
- Build trust through consistency

Bottom line, tone is what makes you distinctive, memorable, and even more trustworthy online.

The key is knowing where YOU want to be on the grid. Then dialing in your writing to hit that sweet spot. And if you don't know what your tone is, you can use AI to help you find your perfect spot on the grid.

Now, let's make this practical.

## The Tone Grid Prompt

Most AI writing tools start off neutral - professional and structured.

But the Tone Grid prompt helps you tweak that. You can push the AI to be more casual, more formal, funnier, or more serious.

Here's a prompt to help you use AI to find your ideal tone:

```plain text
As an AI language model trained on the Tone Grid concept, your task is to rewrite given text samples according to specified tonal parameters.

The Tone Grid consists of two axes:

X-axis: Feel (Loose to Tight)
- 1: Loose
- 10: Tight

Y-axis: Attitude (Irreverent to Professional)
- 1: Irreverent
- 10: Professional

Quadrant Combo Styles:

1. Loose + Irreverent = Playful
2. Loose + Professional = Laid Back
3. Tight + Irreverent = Snappy/Punchy
4. Tight + Professional = Polished

Instructions:
1. Analyze the provided text sample.
2. Consider the specified values for each axis (1-10 scale) and the respective quadrant style combo.
3. Rewrite the text to match the desired tone, maintaining the original message and key points.

Input:

[Original Text]: <Input Your Text>
[Feel (1-10)]: <value>
[Attitude (1-10)]: <value>

Output:
Provide the rewritten text that matches the specified tonal parameters, followed by a brief explanation of the changes made to achieve the desired tone.

```

## Example: Polished

[Original Text]: Your goal as a writer should not be to write a best-selling book. It should be to become known for a niche you own. Then serve that niche dozens of books, digital products, and services over a long time-horizon. And then take the money you make to buy passive-revenue assets.

This version uses a tight, polished style (Feel: 8) with a professional yet slightly bold tone (Attitude: 3).

Play around with it. Try different combinations. Pick your spot on the grid. Refine until it sounds like you.

The same content can be dramatically transformed using different points on the Tone Grid, adjusting both the feel (structure and polish of the copy) and attitude (level of professionalism vs. irreverence) of the writing.

## Key Takeaways

Before we end here are two key things to remember:

1. Good tone doesn't fix bad writing. Get the basics right first.
2. Tone isn't voice. Voice is what you believe. Tone is how you express it.

So, where do you want to land on the Tone Grid?

Find your spot. Own it. Watch your writing go from forgettable to unforgettable.

This is how you engineer tone with AI to write like a human. Like you.

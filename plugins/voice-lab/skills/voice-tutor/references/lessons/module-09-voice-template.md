# Module 9: The Voice Prompt Template

Congratulations! You've made it through all the lessons, and now it's time to bring everything together.

Let's revisit the four key elements that make up your writing voice:

1. **Voice**: The core personality and perspective in your writing
2. **Tone**: The emotional color and attitude in your writing
3. **Style**: The technical aspects of your writing, including word choice and sentence structure
4. **Structure**: How you organize your thoughts and information

These elements don't exist in isolation. They work together, overlapping and intertwining to create your unique writing voice.

## Your Voice Template

```
Write in the style of [Your Name].

Use the following guidelines:

Voice Attributes:
- [List 3-5 key voice attributes, e.g., conversational, direct, empathetic]

Tone Attributes:
- [List 2-3 tone characteristics, e.g., optimistic, sarcastic, passionate]
- Humor Style: [e.g., Dry wit, self-deprecating jokes]

Style Attributes:
- [List 3-5 key style attributes, e.g., uses contractions, employs rhetorical questions, favors active voice]
- Vocabulary Preferences:
  - Power Words: [List 3-5 words you love to use]
  - Avoid Words: [List 3-5 words to avoid]
  - Replacement Words: [List 2-3 word replacements, e.g., 'implement' instead of 'put in place']

Structure Attributes:
- Sentence Length: [e.g., Mix of short (5-10 words) and medium (11-20 words) sentences]
- Paragraph Length: [e.g., 3-4 sentences max]
- Signature Moves: [e.g., Start 20% of sentences with 'But' or 'And', end 25% of paragraphs with questions]

Quirk Quotient:
- [List 2-3 unique quirks, e.g., Use cooking metaphors for complex concepts, reference growing up in rural Iowa]
- Anecdote Frequency: [e.g., Include a personal anecdote every 300 words]

Additional Instructions:
- [Any other specific instructions, e.g., Write from a first-person perspective, address the reader as 'you']
```

Use this template as a starting point for instructing AI to write like you. Fill in each section. Use the mimic and modify method to dial in the different attributes of your voice. With iteration, you'll find that AI can get pretty close to cloning your voice without much editing.

## Voice Examples

Let's take a look at a few voice examples so you can see how all these pieces come together into a distinct writing voice. To highlight the differences, we'll ask Claude to write on the same topic: "3 Time Management Mistakes You're Making as a Solopreneur."

### Example 1: The Pragmatic Problem Solver

```javascript
Write in the style of a Pragmatic Problem Solver.

Use the following guidelines:

Voice Attributes:
- Direct
- Practical
- Analytical
- Solution-oriented
- Concise

Tone Attributes:
- Matter-of-fact
- Confident
- Humor Style: Occasional dry humor, but mostly straightforward

Style Attributes:
- Uses clear, step-by-step explanations
- Employs bullet points and numbered lists frequently
- Favors active voice
- Incorporates relevant examples and analogies

Vocabulary Preferences:
- Power Words: Effective, streamline, optimize, implement, actionable
- Avoid Words: Maybe, hopefully, possibly
- Replacement Words: 'Utilize' instead of 'use', 'crucial' instead of 'important'

Structure Attributes:
- Sentence Length: Primarily short (5-10 words) to medium (11-20 words) sentences
- Paragraph Length: 2-3 sentences max, often using single-sentence paragraphs for emphasis
- Signature Moves: Start each major point with an action verb, end articles with a clear call-to-action

Quirk Quotient:
- Use tool or construction metaphors for problem-solving concepts
- Occasionally reference personal problem-solving experiences
- Anecdote Frequency: Include a brief, relevant anecdote every 500 words

Additional Instructions:
- Address the reader directly as 'you'
- Always include a 'Bottom Line' or 'Key Takeaway' section at the end of each piece
- Anticipate potential obstacles and address them proactively
```

### Example 2: The Witty Commentator

```javascript
Write in the style of The Witty Commentator.

Use the following guidelines:

Voice Attributes:
- Sharp
- Humorous
- Observant
- Culturally aware
- Slightly irreverent

Tone Attributes:
- Satirical
- Playful
- Humor Style: Witty wordplay, pop culture references, occasional self-deprecation

Style Attributes:
- Uses metaphors and similes liberally
- Employs rhetorical questions
- Favors conversational tone with occasional bursts of eloquence
- Incorporates current events and trending topics

Vocabulary Preferences:
- Power Words: Actually, literally, seriously, absolutely, ridiculously
- Avoid Words: Very, really, quite
- Replacement Words: 'Ludicrous' instead of 'silly', 'spectacular' instead of 'great'

Structure Attributes:
- Sentence Length: Mix of snappy short sentences (3-7 words) and longer, more complex ones (15-25 words)
- Paragraph Length: Varied, from single sentences to 3-4 sentences max
- Signature Moves: End 30% of paragraphs with a punchline or twist, start 15% of sentences with "But here's the thing:"

Quirk Quotient:
- Invent absurd hypothetical scenarios to illustrate points
- Occasionally break the fourth wall and address the reader directly
- Anecdote Frequency: Weave in personal anecdotes or "friend of a friend" stories every 250 words

Additional Instructions:
- Write from a first-person perspective
- Use parenthetical asides for extra commentary or jokes
- Incorporate at least one timely pop culture or current event reference per piece
- End each piece with a humorous call-to-action or final quip
```

### Example 3: The Friendly Expert

```javascript
Write in the style of The Friendly Expert.

Use the following guidelines:

Voice Attributes:
- Knowledgeable
- Approachable
- Patient
- Enthusiastic
- Clarifying

Tone Attributes:
- Warm
- Reassuring
- Humor Style: Gentle, occasionally self-deprecating humor

Style Attributes:
- Uses analogies and metaphors to explain complex concepts
- Breaks down information into digestible chunks
- Employs a mix of technical terms and layman's explanations
- Anticipates and addresses common questions

Vocabulary Preferences:
- Power Words: Essentially, fundamentally, notably, interestingly, crucially
- Avoid Words: Obviously, clearly, simply
- Replacement Words: 'Explore' instead of 'look at', 'consider' instead of 'think about'

Structure Attributes:
- Sentence Length: Mix of medium (11-20 words) and longer (21-30 words) sentences, with occasional short sentences for emphasis
- Paragraph Length: 3-5 sentences, with occasional single-sentence paragraphs for important points
- Signature Moves: Start 25% of paragraphs with a question, use "Let's break this down" or "Here's the key thing to remember" to introduce important concepts

Quirk Quotient:
- Use everyday objects or experiences as metaphors for complex ideas
- Occasionally share personal "aha" moments or learning experiences
- Anecdote Frequency: Include a relevant anecdote or case study every 400 words

Additional Instructions:
- Address the reader as "you" to create a sense of conversation
- Use inclusive language like "we" and "us" when discussing the field or topic
- Incorporate rhetorical questions to engage the reader and prompt reflection
- End each piece with a summary of key points and an encouraging note
- Use parentheses to offer additional, friendly asides or clarifications
```

# Module 7: Inject Personality With The "Quirk Quotient"

Your writing quirks are the little idiosyncrasies that make readers think, "Yep, that's definitely Nicolas Cole."
And just like you did when you explored your sentence style, you want to begin by taking a look at what makes your writing different.

- Do you use obscure pop culture references?
- Do you throw in the occasional dad joke?
- Do you relate everything back to your poodle?
- Are you forever quoting Star Wars?

Here are 3 different angles to consider when evaluating quirks:

## 1. Humor

Humor is a big part of personality.
What's your brand of funny?

- Dry
- Witty
- F-bomber

## 2. Metaphors And Analogies

The comparisons you draw reveal a lot about how you think.
Are you more likely to use a sports analogy or a cooking analogy?

## 3. Your Backstory

Your experiences shape your writing.
Maybe you:

- Often reference growing up in a small town
- Frequently draw lessons from your first job
- Regularly allude to your world travels

These personal touches add authenticity to your writing.

Based on your analysis, update your instructions for AI.
Here's a few tips if you're struggling:

1. Ask for feedback. Sometimes others notice our quirks more than we do.
2. Study your most popular pieces. Which quirks are present in your best-received work?
3. Don't force quirks where they don't fit. Authenticity is key.
4. Let your quirks evolve. As you grow and change, so will your writing personality.

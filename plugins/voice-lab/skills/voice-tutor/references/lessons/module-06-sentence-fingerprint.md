# 🤚 Module 6: Your Sentence Fingerprint

You already covered this in the Voice DNA, but it's worth zooming in because how you structure your sentences makes a big difference in how the writing flows and how it's consumed by your readers.

Let's take a closer look.

## Sentence Patterns

Take a few pieces of your writing and break them down.

Look for:

1. **Sentence length**: Do you favor short, punchy sentences? Long, flowing ones? Or a mix?
2. **Sentence structure**: How often do you use simple, compound, or complex sentences? Do you alternate between long and short sentences? Build up to a crescendo with increasingly complex sentences? Or use short sentences for emphasis after longer explanations?
3. **Sentence beginnings**: Do you start with subjects, verbs, or prepositional phrases? Do you lead with "But" or "And" for emphasis?
4. **Transition words**: Which ones do you use most? How often? Are you a "slippery slope" writer?
5. **Rhetorical Questions**: How frequently do you use them?

Every writer has go-to sentence patterns.

And if you don't have a rhythm to your writing yet, this is a handy guide for "musical writing."

### Five Tips for Analyzing Your Sentence Style

1. **Read your writing aloud.** This helps you feel the rhythm and identify awkward structures.
2. **Study writers you admire.** How do they structure their sentences? You might find techniques to incorporate into your own style.
3. **Experiment with new structures.** Your style should evolve as you grow as a writer.
4. **Remember context.** Your sentence style might change depending on whether you're writing a tweet, an email, or a long-form article.
5. **Don't force it.** Your sentence fingerprint should feel natural, not contrived.

## Your Sentence Style Guide

Based on your analysis, create a guide that outlines:

1. **Your ideal sentence length range**
2. **Your preferred mix of sentence structures**
3. **Your signature sentence starters**
4. **Your favorite transition words**
5. **How often you use questions or commands**
6. **Your typical paragraph length and structure**

### Example Style Guide

```
Favor a mix of short (5-10 words) and medium (11-20 words) sentences.

Use complex sentences sparingly.

Start some sentences with 'But' or 'And'.

Use questions to end paragraphs 10% of the time.

Keep paragraphs to 1-3 sentences max.
```

As you work with AI, you'll discover new aspects of your sentence style. You might realize you love using em dashes, or you have a knack for vivid one-sentence descriptions. The point is, refine your style guide as your style evolves.

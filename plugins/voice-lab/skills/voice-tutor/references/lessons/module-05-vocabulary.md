# 🔡 Module 5: Expand (or Tighten) Your Vocabulary

The words you use are a reflection of you—and the audience you'll attract.

For example:
- In high school, maybe you used words like "lit," "bet," or "salty" to fit in.
- Office workers throw around terms like "synergy," "pivot," or "touch base" to sound smart.
- With your Friday night friends, you probably have some inside jokes or catchphrases that only make sense within your circle.

These word choices aren't just random - they're part of your identity in different contexts.

They help you connect with the people around you, whether that's your colleagues, your friends, or your customers. The same is true with your readers. And guess what? AI has its own lingo, that's immediately recognized by most people. Because it sounds like a game-changing, deep diving, crucial robot.

So, when it comes to making AI write like you, fine-tuning your vocabulary is "crucial."

Here's how you can tweak it:

## Step 1: Audit Your Words

Start by analyzing your existing writing.

Look for:
1. Words you use frequently
2. Unique phrases or expressions
3. Industry-specific jargon
4. Words you tend to avoid

Make a list of these words and phrases.

This is your current vocabulary fingerprint.

## Step 2: Create Your Vocabulary Lists

Are there certain types of words you want to use more or less?

Your goal might be to use more technical terms, more colloquial expressions, simplify your language, or to stop using "boom!"

There's no right or wrong answer - it depends on your personal preference.

Based on your audit and goals, create three lists:

1. **Power Words:** Words you love and want to use more often.
2. **Avoid Words:** Words you want to (or you want AI to) use less or eliminate.
3. **Replacement Words:** Better alternatives for words you want to phase out.

For example:
- Power Words: leverage, irresistible, proven
- Avoid Words: very, really, just, game-changer, supercharge
- Replacement Words: "use" instead of "put in place", "important" instead of "crucial"

## Step 3: Train AI to Use Your Vocabulary

Now, incorporate your vocabulary preferences into your AI instructions.

All you have to do is add an instruction to your prompt like:
- Use
- Avoid
- Replace

And follow each command with your list of words.

Easy.

But, don't overdo it. Be stingy with your words and aim for natural integration.

AI will do it's best to follow your instructions. Unfortunately, it likes to see if you're payting attention. So, it's not going to eliminate the need for edits.
